Year-by-Year Cash Flows

CASH IN / CASH OUT
↑
Gross rent
Total rental income before any expenses. Calculated as property value × configured gross yield.
↑
Cash return
Interest or gains generated by money you didn't put into properties, invested in another asset (stocks, fund, etc.).
↓
Mortgage payment (total)
What you pay the bank each year. Calculated with the French amortization system: Payment = Debt × [r×(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n−1], where r = rate/12 and n = months. The payment is fixed, but internally it shifts: at first almost all is interest, at the end almost all is principal.
↓
Mortgage interest
The portion of the payment kept by the bank. Each month = outstanding debt × (rate/12). Decreases progressively as you reduce the debt.
⇄
Principal repaid
The portion of the payment that reduces your debt: Payment − Monthly interest. Not a lost expense — it builds your equity in the property. Grows progressively each year.
↓
Cash return taxes
Tax paid on gains from invested cash. In Spain: 19% up to €6k, 21% on €6–50k, 23% on €50–200k, 27% on €200–300k, 28% >€300k. The net return is what you actually keep and reinvest.
↓
Capital injection ⚠
Extra money you must inject that year because the accumulated cash pool doesn't cover the gap between mortgage and rent. Since the mortgage is paid monthly, the deficit builds throughout the year — modeled as arriving at mid-year. In alternative scenarios, that same amount is invested from that point, generating half a year of return in the arrival year.
⇄
Annual net CF
Net cash flow for the year: rent + net cash return − total mortgage payment. If positive, the year generates liquidity; if negative, you need to provide it.
=
Outstanding debt
Outstanding mortgage balance at the end of that year. Decreases each year according to the French amortization schedule.
=
Net worth
Total value of what you own (properties + accumulated cash) minus the debt you still owe. This is your real wealth at that point.

↑
Gross rent
Rental income from the single house bought outright. With no mortgage, all this money is free.
↑
Cash return
What the surplus money (capital − house price) invested in another asset generates. If you bought the house for less than your capital, the rest works here.
↓
Cash return taxes
Tax on gains from invested capital. Applied to the gross return each year. The remaining amount (net) is what is reinvested and compounded.
⇄
Annual net CF
Rent + net cash return (taxes already deducted). With no mortgage there's no fixed outflow, so this number is always positive or zero.
=
House value
Estimated market price of the property each year, applying the configured appreciation rate to the original purchase price.
=
Accumulated cash
Final value of the money you didn't put into the house, with all compound returns accumulated up to that year.
=
Net worth
House value + accumulated cash. With no debt, all assets are pure net worth. No debt to subtract.
↓
Extra capital (AP→ND)
The same money injected in the leveraged scenario to cover the monthly deficit, here invested instead. Since the deficit accumulates month by month, the money is assumed to arrive at mid-year — generating half a year of return in the arrival year and full return in subsequent years.
